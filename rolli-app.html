Rolli Project Overview

Summary

Rolli is an app that bridges the gap between exercise and fitness apps, designed specifically with skaters in mind. What started as a passion project has now evolved into a full-fledged development effort, complete with a design language, design system, user interface, interactive prototyping, and user testing with fellow skaters and friends.

Problem

    Although there are many fitness apps available for activities like running, biking, walking, and weightlifting, there are few that are dedicated exclusively to passionate skaters. This includes roller-skating, inline skating, and skateboarding.

Goal

    Create an inclusive design system that accommodates all members of the skate community by incorporating engaging and familiar elements to enhance the user experience. Integrate features that connect skaters within community.




Process

Strategy

    • Create an inclusive community around all things skating.
    • Maintain consistency in the app's interface, interactions, and elements.
      • Accessibility: Designing Rolli to be accessible to a wide range of users, including those with disabilities.
        • Using accessible design patterns, such as high-contrast colors, clear typography, and intuitive navigation.

      Discovery: User Research

        • By tapping into the community I am a part of, I was able to identify the diverse range of users who would benefit from using Rolli.
        • These include high school students as well as veteran skaters who are seeking a great place to skate during their lunch break
        • Accessibility: Designing Rolli to be accessible to a wide range of users, including those with disabilities. Consider using accessible design patterns, such as high-contrast colors, clear typography, and intuitive navigation.

      Competitor Analysis

        • I have already analyzed the apps that I currently use on my Apple Watch, as well as fitness apps that I have used in the past.
        • I asked my community what they use to track their skating and how it affects their skate and their experience overall using it.
        • Many applications on the App Store currently only focus on mainstream forms of exercise, such as at-home workouts, cardio, running, weightlifting, biking, and hiking.
